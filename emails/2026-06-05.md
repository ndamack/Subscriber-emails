Subject: Cleopatra Was Closer to Your iPhone Than to the Pyramids

Here's a fact that will break your brain in the best possible way.

Cleopatra — the legendary Egyptian queen, the one with the eyeliner and the asp — lived closer in time to the Moon landing than to the building of the Great Pyramid.

Let that settle in for a second.

The Great Pyramid was built around 2560 BC.

Cleopatra was born around 69 BC.

That's a gap of roughly 2,500 years between her and the people who built her country's most famous landmark.

But between Cleopatra and Neil Armstrong stepping onto the Moon in 1969?

Just under 2,000 years.

The pyramids were already ancient history to Cleopatra.

She was basically a tourist looking up at ruins that had been standing for two and a half millennia.

This means that when you picture ancient Egypt as one big chunk of history — all pyramids and pharaohs and golden cats — you're accidentally smooshing together a timeline longer than the entire modern era.

We think of history as a straight hallway.

It's actually a mansion with wings so far apart they're practically different buildings.

So next time someone dismisses something as "ancient history", remind them: Cleopatra would have called the pyramids ancient history too.

And she lived two thousand years ago.

Time is wild. Have a great week.
