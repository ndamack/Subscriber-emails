Subject: Sharks Existed Before Trees. I Am Not Kidding.

Picture this: you're a shark.

You're gliding through warm, ancient seas.

You're perfectly designed — all muscle and instinct and teeth.

And there are absolutely zero trees anywhere on Earth.

Not one.

Because sharks have been around for roughly 450 million years.

Trees only showed up about 350 million years ago.

That means sharks had a 100-million-year head start on the very concept of a forest.

A hundred million years of ruling the ocean with no shade, no timber, no apples, no atmosphere worth bragging about.

Just water and sharks and the quiet confidence of a creature who knew they'd outlast almost everything.

And here's the kicker: sharks survived the same extinction event that killed the dinosaurs.

The one that wiped out 75% of all life on Earth.

Sharks: completely unbothered.

Meanwhile, we humans have been around for about 300,000 years.

Sharks were already 449.7 million years in by that point.

They've forgotten more about survival than we will ever learn.

Five mass extinctions. Two ice ages. One asteroid the size of a city.

Still here. Still gliding. Still deeply unimpressed by us.

So the next time you feel a flicker of fear watching a nature documentary, just remember: you're not scared of a predator.

You're scared of a time traveler.

See you next week — try not to think about this one too hard.
