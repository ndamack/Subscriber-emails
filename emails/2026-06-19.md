Subject: The Man Who Loved His Invention So Much He Was Buried In It

What if the greatest thing you ever made... became your final resting place?

Fredric Baur was a chemist.

A brilliant one.

In 1966, he invented something so perfectly engineered that it changed snacking forever.

The Pringles can.

Not just the chips — the *can*. That iconic cylindrical tube, designed so precisely that curved, saddle-shaped chips stack perfectly without breaking.

Baur was so proud of this invention that he requested something unusual in his will.

He wanted his ashes buried in one.

When he died in 2008 at age 89, his children stood in a Cincinnati drugstore debating which flavor to choose.

(They went with Original. Respect.)

Then they drove to the cemetery and laid their father to rest — in a Pringles can.

And honestly? That's not weird. That's beautiful.

Because most of us will spend our whole lives searching for the one thing we're meant to create.

Fredric Baur found it. He knew it. And he never let go.

The next time you hear that satisfying *pop* when you open a can... give a little nod.

That sound was engineered by a man who loved his work so much that it literally became part of him.

Once you pop, you can't stop.

Even in death.
