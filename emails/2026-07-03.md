Subject: The 100-year-old veteran who saved a life after death

He survived liberating Nazi concentration camps.

He stood guard at the Nuremberg Trials.

He farmed the land for decades and stayed married for 72 years.

And then, at 100 years old, Dale Steele did one more extraordinary thing.

He gave someone the gift of life.

When Dale passed away earlier this year after a head injury, his family made the decision to donate his organs.

That is when the doctors delivered a fact so wild it sounds like a typo.

His 100-year-old liver — from a man born before commercial aviation was mainstream — was perfectly healthy enough to transplant.

The reason?

Your liver is kind of a miracle machine.

It regenerates its own cells constantly, which means the liver inside a 100-year-old body is effectively only a few years old.

Dale grew vegetables in his garden his entire life.

He worked the land until the very end.

And his liver, biologically speaking, was basically fresh.

Five days after the surgery, the recipient went home.

*Five days.*

Dale Steele served his country before most of us were born.

He witnessed history that shaped the modern world.

And on his final day, he quietly changed someone else's world forever.

The Steele family has not heard from the recipient yet.

But somewhere out there, a person is walking around with a piece of a 100-year-old American hero keeping them alive.

Sometimes the most extraordinary things happen in the quietest ways.
